Information about BMI

  • BMI (Body Mass Index) is a measure for assessing body weight by relating weight to height. Due to the simple measurement, the calculation of the BMI is the most commonly used diagnostic method for classifying body weight. The BMI value determined can be used to quickly determine whether a person is of normal weight, overweight or underweight.
  • Even if BMI does not take into account body fat percentages, it is still the most suitable weight assessment method and provides reliable results for adults who are not particularly large muscle mass or amputated.

The calculated value must then be compared with a value table in order to be able to evaluate whether a person is of normal weight, overweight or underweight.
